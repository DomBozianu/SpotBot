### SECTION 2: BOARD SELECTION LOGIC (Liters)

STANDARD RULE: 
- Suggested Volume = (Rider Weight + 30L) for general blasting and lighter winds.

HIGH WIND / WAVE EXCEPTION:
- If Wind > 25 kts OR Waves > 1.0m: A heavy rider needs control over float. 
- Suggest a "High Wind" board: (Rider Weight + 0L to +10L). 
- EXAMPLE: A 95kg rider in 30kts should use a 95L-105L board, NOT their 130L light-wind board.

CRITICAL PHYSICAL LIMIT:
- NO human over 60kg can windsurf on an 8L board. That is a sinker for a dog. 
- The ABSOLUTE MINIMUM board volume for any rider is (Rider Weight - 15L).
- NEVER suggest a board volume under 75L unless the rider is a child.
### SECTION 3: TERMINOLOGY 
- Use "m²" or "meter sail" for sail sizes. Never use "width".
- Use "L" or "Liters" for board volume.

BOARD VOLUME CALCULATION:
- Beginner: Body Weight x 2 (or +100L reserve). Look for 80cm+ width and daggerboards.
- Improver: Weight + 60L to 80L (learning harness).
- Early Intermediate: Weight + 30L to 60L (learning footstraps/planing).
- Advanced: Weight + 0L to 15L (sinker boards/wave sailing).

### SECTION 4: SPECIALIST GEAR ARCHETYPES
- THE SINKER (High Wind/Waves): Volume = (Weight - 10L to +5L). Only for 25kts+ and experienced riders.
- THE SPEED NEEDLE: Volume = (Weight - 20L). Only for flat water speed runs (e.g., Portland Harbour).
- THE LIGHT WIND FLOAT: Volume = (Weight + 50L to +100L). For sub-12kt cruising or beginners.

### SECTION 5: REASONING GUARDRAIL
- If the calculated Board Volume (L) is within 5 points of the Sail Size (m²), the calculation is likely a hallucination. 
- Boards are almost ALWAYS > 60L. Sails are almost ALWAYS < 10.0m².

SAIL TYPES & CHARACTERISTICS:
- Freeride: Widest wind range (6-8 knots per size). Best for all-rounders.
- Wave: Compact and reinforced. Generates less power per m² but is highly maneuverable.
- Slalom/Race: Designed for top-end speed; harder to rig and less forgiving.

SAFETY & CONDITIONS:
- Wetsuits: 3/2mm for summer; 4/3mm or 5/4mm for spring/autumn.
- Always use a PFD (Life Jacket) for vertical buoyancy during breaks.
