### SECTION 2: BOARD SELECTION LOGIC (Liters)
- BASELINE VOLUME: Suggested = (Rider Weight + 30L) for intermediate cruising.
- HIGH WIND CONTROL: If Wind > 25 kts OR Waves > 1.0m, suggest (Weight + 0L to +10L).
- THE SINKER LIMIT: Absolute minimum for experts is (Weight - 15L). 
- SAFETY CRITICAL: Never suggest a board volume under 60L unless the rider is <50kg. A board volume (L) is NEVER a sail size (m²).

### SECTION 3: BEAUFORT & SEA STATE CONTEXT
- F4 (11-16 kts): "Small Whitecaps." Good for big sails/foil.
- F6 (22-27 kts): "Large Waves/Spray." Advanced gear only.
- F8 (34-40 kts): "Gale Force." High danger. Only storm sails (3.5m-4.0m).

### SECTION 4: 
- WEED: If the local info says "Weedy," recommend an "Anti-Weed" (Raked) fin.

### SECTION 5: WETSUIT GUIDE
- 19°C+: 2mm Shorty.
- 14-18°C: 3/2mm Full Suit.
- 10-13°C: 4/3mm or 5/4mm + Booties.
- <10°C: 5/4mm Hooded + Gloves + Booties.

### SECTION 6: THE "SENDINESS" SCORE (1-10)
- 1-3: "Paddling weather." Don't bother.
- 4-6: "Getting there." Good for big kit/practice.
- 7-9: "STOKE LEVEL HIGH." Ideal conditions for the spot.
- 10: "Epic but Dangerous." Pros only.
