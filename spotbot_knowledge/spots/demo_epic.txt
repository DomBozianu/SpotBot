Spot Name: Epic Spot (Presentation Demo Spot)

The Vibe & Disciplines

    Disciplines: Wave Sailing, Freestyle, Slalom — this spot does it all.

    Skill Level: Intermediate to Expert. When it's nuking like today, you need to know what you're doing.

    Character: A legendary exposed headland that funnels SW swells into a perfect cross-shore ramp.
    When the Atlantic fires up, this place goes world-class. The locals know it. Now you do too.

Conditions & Wind Directions

    Ideal Wind: SW and W. The headland geometry turns a SW into a perfect cross-shore at the launch.

    Workable Directions: NW is cross-onshore and still rideable. SE is offshore — do not go out alone.

    Tides: Best 2 hours either side of mid-tide. Low tide exposes a reef on the north end — stay south.

    Tide Warning: Spring tides push a strong rip along the headland. Check before you launch.

Launch Sites, Parking & Facilities

    Parking: Gravel car park at the top of the access track. Tight in summer — arrive early.

    The Kit-Carry: Short but steep. Concrete slipway at the south end makes it manageable.

    Facilities: No permanent facilities on site. Nearest café is 3 miles inland. Bring water.

Hazards & Safety

    The Reef: Exposed at low tide on the north side. Marked by a yellow buoy — stay south of it.

    Rip Current: Runs along the headland on big spring tides. If you get caught, sail downwind and come in on the south beach.

    Rocks: Submerged boulders 50m off the headland. Visible at low tide, invisible at high. Know the spot.

    Offshore Risk: SE and E winds look tempting but push you out fast. Locals only in those directions.

Local Knowledge

    The sandbar shifts every winter storm. What worked last season may not work now — ask a local.

    The pub in the village does a legendary post-session pie. You've earned it.
