**Marazion Beach Windsurfing Guide**

**Location:** Marazion Beach, Marazion, Cornwall

**Suggested Tide ID:** 5240 (Penzance)

**General Info:**

Marazion Beach is a popular windsurfing spot on the south coast of Cornwall, situated near the iconic St Michael's Mount. The beach is suitable for all levels, from beginners to advanced windsurfers.

**Suitability:**

* Beginner: Yes, the beach is ideal for learning to windsurf, especially at low to mid-tide.
* Intermediate: Yes, the beach offers a range of conditions, including waves and flat water.
* Advanced: Yes, the beach can produce high-quality waves and strong winds.

**Wind Directions:**

* Best wind directions: W, E, SE, SW
* Suitable wind directions: NW, S, SSW, SE
* Avoid wind directions: NE, N

**Tidal Range:**

* Small tidal range, making it suitable for windsurfing at most times.

**Shelving Rate:**

* Slight shelving at mid-low tide, making it ideal for beginners.

**Features:**

* The beach performs well in most wind directions, making it a versatile spot.
* Jumping can be great on both tacks, and the wave riding can be good too.
* The beach is sheltered from powerful W and NW swells, making it a great spot for entry-level windsurfers.

**Considerations:**

* Be aware of the shore dump at high tide, which can be a gear-trasher.
* Avoid kitesurfing near the railway track, especially 2 hours either side of high tide.
* Watch out for thieves in the car park.
* Be mindful of the summer months when the beach is popular with swimmers and bathers.

**Parking and Amenities:**

* Parking: Pay and display car park near Jordan's café, as well as free parking along the top of the seawall.
* Amenities: Toilets, café, and pub nearby.

**Getting There:**

* Follow the signs just outside Penzance on the A30.

**Accommodation:**

* Plenty of B&B options in Penzance.
