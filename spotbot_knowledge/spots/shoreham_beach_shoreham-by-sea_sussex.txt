**Shoreham Beach Windsurf Guide**

Located in Shoreham-by-Sea, Sussex, Shoreham Beach is a popular spot for windsurfers, offering a large shingle beach with consistent sea breezes and a variety of wave conditions.

**Best Wind Direction:**

South West (SW) winds are ideal for Shoreham Beach, with cross-onshore to side shore conditions. West winds are also suitable, although they can be slightly offshore.

**Tide:**

Shoreham-by-Sea experiences semi-diurnal tides, with two high tides and two low tides each day. The best windsurfing conditions are typically found at low tide, when the beach is exposed and the waves are larger. However, the beach is still suitable for windsurfing at high tide, with a shorebreak that can be challenging for beginners.

**Suggested Tide ID:**

Based on the nearest port, Shoreham-by-Sea, the suggested tide ID is 5303.

**Wave Conditions:**

Shoreham Beach offers a variety of wave conditions, from small waves to head-high breaking waves. The waves are typically cross-onshore, making it an ideal spot for bump and jump and freestyle windsurfing.

**Launch Point:**

The main launch point is located at the East end of a large car park next to the Widewater Lagoon. The car park charges are £1.50 per day, and there are also toilets and showers available.

**Safety:**

Be aware of the groynes and rip at high tide, and take care when launching and landing. The beach is generally safe at low tide, but it's always a good idea to check the conditions before heading out.

**Accommodation:**

There is one B&B on the beach, but there are also plenty of options in nearby Brighton and Worthing.

**Getting There:**

Shoreham Beach is easily accessible by car, with signposted parking areas and a large car park. The nearest airport is Shoreham Airport, which is a short drive from the beach.

**Rating:**

Shoreham Beach is suitable for intermediate to advanced windsurfers, with a rating of 4 out of 5. The beach offers a variety of wave conditions and consistent sea breezes, making it an ideal spot for experienced windsurfers.

**Tips:**

* Check the tide times and wind direction before heading out.
* Be aware of the groynes and rip at high tide.
* Take care when launching and landing.
* Enjoy the consistent sea breezes and varied wave conditions.

Overall, Shoreham Beach is a great spot for windsurfers, offering a variety of wave conditions and consistent sea breezes. With its easy access and range of amenities, it's an ideal destination for windsurfers of all levels.
