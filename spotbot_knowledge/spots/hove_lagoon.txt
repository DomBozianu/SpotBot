**Hove Lagoon Windsurfing Guide**

**General Info**

Hove Lagoon is a premier windsurfing spot located in Brighton, UK. It's a great place for all levels, from beginners to pros. The lagoon offers sheltered flat-water conditions, ideal for learning and practicing windsurfing techniques.

**Suitability**

* Beginners: The lagoon is perfect for learning windsurfing, with calm and shallow waters.
* Intermediate: The summer sea breezes produce flat to choppy water sailing ideal for blasting and freestyle.
* Advanced: The lagoon can get challenging when the wind picks up, but it's still a great spot for experienced windsurfers to practice their skills.

**Best Conditions**

* Wind: Summer sea breezes (WSW)
* Tide: Low to mid-tide
* Wave: Small to medium waves

**Features**

* The lagoon is about the size of a football pitch and is only waist deep all the way across.
* The surrounding beach offers a variety of conditions, from flat water to waves.
* The lagoon is surrounded by a line of buoys, making it a safe and controlled environment.

**Hazards**

* An old wooden groyne to the left of the launch area, only exposed on a spring low tide.
* A few dinghy sailors in the summer.

**Directions**

* Take the M23 or A27 to Brighton, then follow the signs to Hove Lagoon Watersports.
* Parking is available on site, but it can get busy.

**Facilities**

* Toilets and showers on site
* Windsurfing shop nearby (Surfladle, 15-minute drive away)
* Food and drink available at the Hove Lagoon Clubhouse
* Plenty of pubs and restaurants in the surrounding area

**Tide ID: 5404**

This is based on the nearest port, Brighton, and the tide times for Hove Lagoon. The suggested tide ID is for a mid-tide condition, which is ideal for windsurfing in the lagoon.

**Risk Assessments**

* Injury from falling off windsurf simulator: 1/2/2 (simulator to be used on grass area, instructor supervision required)
* Injury from colliding with edge of lagoon: 3/3/9 (clients briefed to drop sails and drop to knees if near edge of lagoon)
* Injury from falling off board into lagoon: 4/2/8 (clients advised to spread their fall into lagoon and wear correct footwear)

**Tips and Reminders**

* Always wear correct footwear and follow the instructions of the instructors.
* Be aware of the surrounding buoys and keep a safe distance.
* Respect other water users, including dinghy sailors and other windsurfers.

Overall, Hove Lagoon is an excellent spot for windsurfing, with a variety of conditions and a safe and controlled environment. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced windsurfer, you'll find something to suit your skills and preferences.
