Spot Name: Kimmeridge Bay (K-Bay)  

The Vibe & Disciplines  

    Disciplines: Wave sailing (world-class), surfing, and SUP.

    Skill Level: Intermediate to Expert only; serious wave-riding territory.  

    Character: A legendary, isolated reef break with a "stronghold" feel for windsurfers. No beach break; it's all about the reefs.  

The Three Main Breaks  

    The Bay: Central, off the slipway. Best in S or SW; flattens out at low tide.  

    The Ledges: Under the cliffs to the east. Best for jumping (W to NW) or wave riding (SE). Intermediates should head here.  

    The Bench: To the west. A world-class port-tack right-hander for experts only.  

Conditions & Wind Directions  

    Ideal Wind: NW, W, SW, S, and E.

    Waves: Best after a long SW blow when the wind veers W or NW.  

    Tides: Can be sailed at all times, but the reef is best at mid to high tide on the "push".  

    Tide Quirk: Similar to Portland, it has a strange "double low tide" that can catch you out.  

Launch Sites, Parking & Facilities  

    Access: Via a privately owned toll road. Charges range from £3 to £6 per car, often higher if you have a roof rack with kit.  

    Main Car Park: Large grass area overlooking the bay. In summer, you may be redirected to a top car park, requiring a short walk down.  

    Kit-Carry: Usually a 5-minute walk downhill from the main car park to the shore.  

    Facilities: Toilets available at the slipway/car park area. No showers on-site. A café and pub are located in the village (about 5-10 mins away).  

Hazards & Safety  

    The Firing Range: The Bench is on an MOD range. If the Red Flags are flying, it is a strict no-go area; squadies will dissuade you from landing.  

    Rocks & Reefs: The bottom is jagged limestone. Launching and landing is tricky, especially at low tide—"watch out for the conger eels" on the ledges.  

    Cliffs: Danger of rockfalls; do not rig or sit directly under the cliffs.  

    "The Washing Ledge": A notorious problem area in the SE corner; stay well upwind in W and SW winds.
