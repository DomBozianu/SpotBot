**The Bluff, Hayle Windsurfing Guide**

The Bluff, Hayle, Cornwall, UK

**Suitability:** Advanced windsurfers

**Beach Faces:** NW

**Best Wind:** W, SW, WSW

**Tidal Range:** Large

**Shelving Rate:** Sloping with sand bars

**Wave:** Excellent for wave sailing

**Freestyle:** Good for freestyle windsurfing

**Speed:** Suitable for speed windsurfing

**Beginner:** Not recommended for beginners due to strong currents and shallow water

**The Bluff:** Located on the south-western edge of Three Mile Beach, right at the mouth of the River Hayle. The Bluff works best at low water levels, when you will find a wide sandy beach without rocks.

**Suggested Tide ID:** 5140 (based on the nearest port, Hayle)

**Directions to The Bluff:**

1. Take the turning on the A30 for Hayle and drive towards the centre of town.
2. Head to the Bluff In or The Hayle Beach Car Park if accessing the Bluff from the North via Lethlean Lane and Churchtown Road.
3. Alternatively, access the Bluff from the South via Harvey Towans Car Park accessed via North Quay.

**Facilities:**

* Pub
* Toilets
* Restaurants
* Shops
* Car parking (for a fee)

**Considerations:**

* The Bluff is a river mouth, so consider the extra pull on an outgoing tide.
* At low tide, the wave breaks very hollow onto a sandbar, which can be a proper kit breaker.
* There is a longish tricky walk down to the beach and over a rocky area, it is best to carry your kit down and then rig up on the beach.

**Tips and Tricks:**

* The wind can funnel down the river to give you an extra boost, the added effect of the funnelling means you get some sessions in just when you were not suspecting.
* The spot can provide epic conditions for both surfing and windsurfing.
* Be aware of the strong current on an ebbing tide, which can take your board out to sea quickly.
* Watch out as high tide arrives as the beach at the bottom of the access path disappears.

**Kitesurfing Restrictions:**

* There is a code of conduct, and a permit and insurance is required.
* The prime winds for kitesurfing are from the NE, N, NW and W (Westerly can be gusty for kitesurfing and so better to move up to Gwithian).
* Look out for boats, beach users, a strong current, telegraph poles and the shallow sand bar as the tide comes in.

**Permits:**

* Permits are free for members of the KKC, £10 for non-members, and run from 1st April each year.
* Non-BKSA members may purchase a tag for £20 provided they have valid liability insurance of 5 million pounds.

**Best Time to Visit:**

* The best time to visit is spring or autumn, when the wind is more likely to be suitable for windsurfing.

**Wetsuit:**

* A 4/3 or 5/3 wetsuit usually fits.

**Shoes:**

* Shoes are not absolutely necessary at most spots as there are fine sandy beaches in many places.

**Accommodation:**

* There are various accommodation options available in the area, including Tom's Holidays - Chalet Park above The Bluff, and Forever Cornwall/Gwithian, which has many offerings in the eastern part of St Yves Bay.
