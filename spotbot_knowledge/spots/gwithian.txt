Spot Name: Gwithian (St Ives Bay)

The Vibe & Disciplines  

    Disciplines: Premier Wave Sailing (Down-the-line), Kitesurfing, and Surfing.  

    Skill Level: Intermediate to Expert. Not a beginner spot due to significant swell and the "shore dump."  

    Character: A massive sandy beach backed by dunes and cliffs. It catches the most swell in St Ives Bay and can get "mast-high" and powerful.  

Conditions & Wind Directions  

    Ideal Wind: SW and SSW are the "classic" directions for port-tack jumping and backside wave riding.  

    Workable Directions: NE provides good cross-shore conditions. N and NW are cross-onshore.  

    Tides: Best sailed 3 to 4 hours either side of low water.  

    Tide Warning: The beach disappears roughly 2 hours before high tide. High tide causes a "vicious shore dump" and backwash off the cliffs.  

Launch Sites, Parking & Facilities

    Parking: Large "Pay & Display" car park at the top of the cliffs overlooking the beach. Plentiful space, but busy in summer.  

    The "Kit-Carry" (Crucial):

        The 'Goat Track': A very steep, slippery cliff descent directly in front of the car park. Fast but sketchy with kit.  

        The Path: A longer, less severe sloping path leads towards the lifeguard hut with concrete steps to the beach.  

    Facilities: Toilets and a café (Sunset Surf or similar) are located right by the main car park.  

Hazards & Safety

    Rocks: Submerged rocks directly in front of the car park and to the North (Strap Rocks). When the tide reaches these rocks, it's time to head in or move towards Godrevy.  

    The River/Godrevy Rip: A strong rip current exists near the Red River mouth at the Godrevy end. Avoid this area during heavy rain due to potential pollution.  

    Spring Tides: The tide comes in extremely fast; it is easy to get cut off against the cliffs.  

    Zoning: Respect lifeguard flags and surfing zones during summer months.
