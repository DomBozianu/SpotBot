Here's a windsurfing guide for Calshot Beach:

**Calshot Beach Windsurfing Guide**

**Location:** Calshot Beach, Southampton, UK

**Suggested Tide ID:** 4-5 (based on Southampton Water)

**Suitability:** Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced

**Beach Faces:** SE

**Best Wind:** N, NE, SW

**Tidal Range:** Small

**Shelving Rate:** Moderate shelving rate at high tide, flat water lagoon at low tide

**Description:** Calshot Beach is a great spot for windsurfers of all levels, with a variety of conditions to suit different abilities. The beach faces SE, making it ideal for northerly winds, which blow straight into the bay on the back side of the spit, providing safe sailing and flat water blasting. The pond behind the spit is a brilliant flat water spot for freestyle, accessible for kite surfing between November and March, and for windsurfers all year round.

**Features:**

* Shallow water at mid-tide, making it perfect for beginners
* Great freeride conditions at all states of tide
* Prevailing south westerlies blow cross shore, but don't expect any wave sailing due to the shelter from the Isle of Wight
* Totally sheltered lagoon area for beginners
* Access to the pond behind the spit for flat water freestyle and speed sailing

**Hazards:**

* Tidal flow moving around the spit, making it hard to hold your ground once the sand bar is covered
* Small craft traffic in the channel, including rescue boats and ferries
* Shallow sand bars and pebbles, requiring boots to navigate

**Facilities:**

* Toilets and showers available at the Calshot Activities Centre
* Windsurfing shop and tuition available at Solent Sailboards
* Evening restaurant and beach cafe on site
* Parking available at the Activity Centre or public car parks

**Membership:**

* Club membership is mandatory for kitesurfers, available from Calshot Activities Centre or 24-7 Boardsports for £65 per year
* Windsurfers can park and launch at the Activity Centre for £6 per day, or use the public Pay and Display car park

**Tips:**

* Check the tide times and ABP tide table for optimal conditions
* Be aware of the shallow sand bars and pebbles, and wear boots to navigate
* Respect the local sailing community and follow the rules and regulations

Overall, Calshot Beach is a great spot for windsurfers of all levels, offering a variety of conditions and facilities to suit different abilities. With its sheltered lagoon and flat water, it's an ideal spot for beginners and freestylers, while the prevailing south westerlies make it perfect for freeride and speed sailing.
