**Daymer Bay Windsurf Guide**

**Location:** Daymer Bay, Cornwall

**Suggested Tide ID:** 5404 (based on Padstow, the nearest standard port)

**General Info:**

Daymer Bay is a beautiful beach located on the Camel estuary, backed by sand dunes and a golf course. The water is shallow and ideal for bathing, making it a great spot for windsurfers of all levels. The bay offers a variety of conditions, from flat water to waves, and can be sailed in different wind directions.

**Windsurfing Ratings:**

Daymer Bay is suitable for beginners in summer through to advanced wave riders. The bay can be a good place to learn all about waves, and the estuary provides a unique opportunity to sail wind against tide, staying on a broad reach all day.

**Best Conditions:**

* Wind direction: North, North-west, and South
* Tide: High tide for sailing wind against tide, and low tide for best speed sailing conditions
* Wave size: Head to logo high waves, mast high on the 'doom bar'
* Current: Up to 8 knots, especially when the water is running out

**Facilities:**

* Toilets: Yes, on site
* Shower: No
* Windsurfing Shop: Yes, right in the car park selling spares and other bits & bobs
* Food: Yes, at cafe in car park
* Parking: Free in the winter, £5 in the summer
* Pub: Go to Polzeath for posh grub with a view

**Tips and Precautions:**

* Be aware of the strong outgoing tide, which can reach up to 8 knots
* Stay 20 meters away from the cliffs
* Be mindful of motor craft in the summer
* Not many swimmers/inflatables due to tide, they all go to Polzeath beach
* Kitesurfers, but not on early summer mornings
* Single men, you might find the windsurfing woman of your dreams here!

**Directions:**

Head for Polzeath, then drive through the town and up the massively steep Hill. When you get to the top, drive for about 1.5 miles and look out for a subtle right turn. The car park is down the end of this road.

**Tide Times:**

Check the 7-day tide forecast for Daymer Bay, which can be found on the UK Hydrographic Office website.
