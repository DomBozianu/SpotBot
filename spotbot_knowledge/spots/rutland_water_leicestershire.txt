**Windsurf Guide to Rutland Water, Leicestershire**

Located just 20 miles east of Leicester, Rutland Water is a popular spot for windsurfers in the Midlands. The north shore of the lake, facing south, is the most popular spot, with a west-north-westerly wind blowing down the north arm of the lake on good days.

**Best Wind Conditions:**

* WNW, E, S, SW

**Hazards:**

* Fishing boats, which tend to use other parts of the reservoir
* The Rutland Bell tourist cruise boat in the summer

**Tide Information:**

* Suggested Tide ID: 5401 (nearest port: Boston)

**Tips and Tricks:**

* The lake's flat and shallow sailing area makes it ideal for windsurfing, especially in the summer months.
* Be aware of the potential for choppy waters in the winter months.
* The Rutland Water is a great spot for all levels of windsurfers, with plenty of space to practice and improve your skills.

**Additional Information:**

* Rutland Water is home to the National Watersports Festival, which takes place every summer.
* The festival features demo's and lessons from industry legends, as well as light wind racing and SUP yoga sessions.
* The lake is a mecca for youth sailing and all-level windsurfing, with no jet skis or kite-surfers allowed.

**Getting There:**

* Rutland Water is easily accessible by car, with plenty of parking available.
* The lake is also within easy reach of Loughborough and Nottingham.

**Conclusion:**

Rutland Water is a must-visit spot for windsurfers in the UK. With its ideal wind conditions, flat and shallow sailing area, and plenty of space to practice, it's the perfect spot to improve your skills and enjoy the thrill of windsurfing. So grab your kit and head to Rutland Water for an unforgettable windsurfing experience!
