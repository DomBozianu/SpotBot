Spot Name: Rhosneigr (Traeth Llydan / Broad Beach)

The Vibe & Disciplines
    Disciplines: Wave, Freestyle, Freeride
    Skill Level: Intermediate to Expert (Advanced on big swell)
    Character: A world-class wave sailing playground that works in almost any wind direction. Horseshoe bay that accelerates swell up the Irish Channel.

Conditions & Wind Directions
    Ideal Wind: S, SW (Cross-shore), NW (Cross-onshore)
    Workable Directions: Almost anything, but avoid pure East (Offshore/Gusty).
    Tides: Best sailed 2 hours either side of High Tide for the best waves.
    Tide Warning: Large tidal range; the beach changes character completely from low to high.

Launch Sites, Parking & Facilities
    Parking: Pay and display car park near the beach; gets very busy in summer.
    Facilities: Funsport shop in the village for spares/lessons, several cafes, and public toilets.

Hazards & Safety
    - Hidden Rocks: Massive rocks are hidden at high tide. Check the beach at low tide to memorize their positions.
    - The River: Watch for the river mouth at the right of the bay (Traeth Crigyll) which can create strong currents.
    - Strong Rips: Be wary of rips when the swell is large; kit recovery can be difficult.

Local Knowledge
    - "The Clock": A famous rock outcrop in the bay used as a landmark.
    - The bay is safe for learning when flat, but becomes a serious wave arena when the Atlantic swell rolls in.
