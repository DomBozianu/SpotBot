**Windsurf Guide for Ray, Southend-on-Sea, Essex**

**Getting There**

The Ray is located in Southend-on-Sea, Essex, and can be accessed by car or public transportation. The nearest postcode is SS0 8JH. Parking is available at East Beach, but be aware that there is limited parking at The Ray.

**Best Winds**

The best winds for The Ray are from the South (S) or South-East (SE) direction, which is ideal for blasting up and down on big sail and small boards or sailing Freestyle kit. You can also expect long runs along the western end of The Ray towards Leigh-on-Sea.

For speed sailing, the best winds are from the South-West (SW) or West (W) direction, which is great for blasting along the eastern end of The Ray passed the dogleg. The fastest wind direction for speed sailing is towards the Western end of The Ray on a SW swinging West wind.

**Tide Information**

The tide is crucial for windsurfing at The Ray. The best time to sail is around 3 hours either side of low tide. It's essential to be aware of the shallow banks, which move around every year, so it's best to sail at low tide only until you know where the shallow banks are.

**Safety**

When sailing at The Ray, be aware of the shallow banks and the pier end, which tends to shallow at low tide. Also, watch out for strong currents between the 3rd and 4th hour before and after high tide.

**Tips and Tricks**
