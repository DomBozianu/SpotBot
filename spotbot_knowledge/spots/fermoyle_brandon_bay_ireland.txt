**Fermoyle Windsurf Guide**

Located on the stunning Brandon Bay in Ireland, Fermoyle is a world-class windsurfing spot that offers a unique combination of consistent winds and varied swell conditions. As one of the best windsurfing destinations in Europe, Fermoyle is a must-visit for any windsurfer looking to experience the best of what the Atlantic coast has to offer.

**Best Wind Directions:**

* West wind: Fermoyle is best suited for west winds, with a minimum of 25 knots required for optimal conditions.
* NW wind cross-on: At low tide, Fermoyle can also be sailed at NW wind cross-on when the reefs on the Maharees are dry.

**Swell Directions:**

* North or northwest: Fermoyle receives swell from the north or northwest, making it a great spot for wave sailing.
* Sheltered by Brandon Head: The bay's unique shape provides shelter from the swell, making it a great spot for all levels of wave sailors.

**Tide:**

* Okay at all tides: Fermoyle is suitable for windsurfing at all tides, making it a great spot for those who want to sail at different times of the day.
* Great for jumping and cross-wave riding: The consistent winds and varied swell conditions make Fermoyle an ideal spot for jumping and cross-wave riding.

**Tips and Tricks:**

* Be aware of the wind direction and swell conditions before heading out to Fermoyle.
* Take advantage of the bay's unique shape to sail at different spots and angles.
* Don't be afraid to try new things and experiment with different techniques.

**Getting There:**

* Fermoyle is located on the Dingle Peninsula in County Kerry, Ireland.
* The nearest airport is Kerry Airport (KIR), which is approximately 30 minutes from Fermoyle.
