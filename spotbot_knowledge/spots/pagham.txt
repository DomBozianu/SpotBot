**Pagham Windsurf Guide**

**Location:** Pagham, West Sussex, UK

**Best Wind Directions:** S, SW, NE, E

**Best Tide:** Low tide for beginners, higher tides for intermediate to advanced riders

**Suggested Tide ID:** 5401 (based on the nearest port, Bognor Regis)

**Beach Conditions:**

* The beach is a shingle beach with a sandbar that appears at low tide, creating a fun but not powerful wave for jumping.
* At high tide, the beach is steeper and the waves can make launching difficult.
* The shorebreak is deceptive, so be quick!

**Currents:**

* Be careful of the currents near the river mouth and the water movement around the wavebreak.
* Avoid swimming or launching here unless you understand the risks.

**Launch Points:**

* The 'new' shingle beach at Pagham now offers respectable conditions more appropriate for free-riding and enjoying the wind swell.
* Launch opposite the Pagham Yacht Club for safety reasons, unless you are looking to specifically get caught in the river flow.

**Features:**

* Pagham is side shore in a SW or a NE, and is also good in an S when few places on the south coast are.
* Close to the beach is often flat and great for gybing and freestyle practice.
* Pagham is one of the few places you can get a windsurf in in a North Easterly.

**Parking:**

* Pay & Display near Sailing Club
* Free at the Spit Car Park

**Accommodation:**

* B&B's and hotels in Bognor

**Amenities:**

* Water Quality: Not specified
* Suitable for: Beginner - lower tides, Intermediate to advanced - higher tides

**Rating:**

* Our Rating: Not specified

**Tips:**

* Beginners, freeriders, and freestylers will love the flatter water at low tide.
* Avoid high tide at Hayling seafront, which is known for its shoredump.
* Portland is a great spot for freestyle, speed sailing, and slalom, but it's not specified if it's suitable for windsurfing.

**Tide Times:**

* Check the tide times for Pagham to plan your session.

**Safety:**

* Be aware of the currents and water movement around the wavebreak.
* Avoid swimming or launching here unless you understand the risks.

**Conclusion:**

Pagham is a great spot for windsurfing, with a variety of conditions and features to suit different levels of riders. Be aware of the currents and water movement, and plan your session according to the tide times.
