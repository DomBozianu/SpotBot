**Bigbury Beach Windsurf Guide**

**Location:** Bigbury-on-Sea, Devon, England

**Best Wind Directions:** W, NE, SW, S, WNW

**Best Tide:** Medium to high tide

**Suggested Tide ID:** 4.5 (based on the nearby port of Plymouth)

**Beach Description:** Bigbury Beach is a crescent-shaped bay with a popular launch point at Bigbury-on-Sea, offering excellent access and a car park nearby. The beach faces south, making it suitable for a wide variety of wind directions.

**Windsurfing Conditions:**

* **Wave:** Excellent
* **Freestyle:** Good
* **Speed:** Good

**Tips and Tricks:**

* Bigbury offers some of the best port tack wavesailing in England, particularly in easterly winds with a big south swell.
* The beach is suitable for all levels, from beginners to advanced windsurfers.
* The waves can get huge in the estuary, making it a challenging spot for experienced windsurfers.
* Be careful when launching and landing, as the beach can get busy in the summer months.
* The best beach is just below Bigbury, which is quite small and frequented by windsurfers at medium to high tide.

**Launch Points:**

* Bigbury-on-Sea: The most popular launch point, with excellent access and a car park nearby.
* Bantham: A good alternative launch point, but be aware that it's in the lee of Bigbury, making it gusty in NW winds.

**Hazards:**

* Rips and strong currents can occur when the swell is big.
* Be careful when launching and landing, as the beach can get busy in the summer months.
* Dogs are not allowed on the beach.

**Facilities:**

* Car park
* Toilets
* Lifeguards on duty in the summer months
* Cafe/restaurant
* Shop
* Pub nearby

**Accommodation:**

* There are no campsites or accommodation at Bigbury itself, but there are nearby options in the surrounding area.

**Getting There:**

* The beach is accessible by car, with a large pay-and-display car park available.
* The nearest town is Kingsbridge, which has a range of amenities and accommodation options.

**Conclusion:**

Bigbury Beach is a popular windsurfing spot, offering a wide variety of conditions and a beautiful location. With its crescent-shaped bay and south-facing beach, it's suitable for windsurfers of all levels. However, be aware of the strong currents and rips, and take care when launching and landing.
