**Joss Bay Windsurf Guide**

Located on the east coast of Kent, Joss Bay is a popular spot for windsurfers and kitesurfers alike. The beach is exposed to winds from the north through to the south east, making it an ideal location for those looking to harness the power of the wind.

**Access and Launching**

Access to the beach is easy, with parking available near the surf school on the southern end of the beach. The beach is large and sandy, making it perfect for launching and landing your board. However, be aware of the tourists as the beach gets very busy in the summer.

**Spot Information**

The beach is a large sandy beach with a rocky reef at the northern end, which becomes exposed in low tides. Joss Bay can be used at all states of the tide, but for beginners, it's best at mid to low tide when there are large shallow areas to stand in the water.

**Wind and Tide**

The prevailing south westerly wind on the south coast is offshore at this location, making it an ideal spot for windsurfers. The best conditions are reported when a north swell combines with an offshore wind direction from the west-southwest.

**Tips and Precautions**

* Be aware of the tourists and other beach users, especially during peak season.
* Watch out for the rocky reef at the northern end of the beach, which can be exposed at low tide.
* Make sure to check the wind and tide conditions before heading out.
* Be prepared for strong winds and rough seas.
