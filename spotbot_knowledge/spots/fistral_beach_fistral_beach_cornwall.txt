Here is a windsurfing guide for Fistral Beach, Fistral Beach, Cornwall:

**Fistral Beach Windsurfing Guide**

**Location:** Fistral Beach, Cornwall, England, UK

**Best Time:** Mid tide to just before high tide, with spring high tide also suitable if there is enough swell. Low tide or spring low tide at the north end of the beach is also a good option.

**Ideal Wind:** SW winds

**Wave Height:** At least 1.5 meters guaranteed when the sea is calm

**Tide:** Suggested Tide ID: 4-5 (mid tide to just before high tide)

**Difficulty:** Suitable for experts only, with waves over mast high suitable for experienced riders only

**Parking:** Pay & Display parking available, with some car parks accepting credit cards and others requiring coins or mobile payment

**Amenities:** Usual seaside town amenities available in Newquay

**Tips:**

* Be aware of the local surfers and respect their territory
* Be careful of the severe rip in a flowing tide (incoming) that takes you from left to right onto the rocks under the Headland Hotel
* Consider renting a 4 mm wetsuit as the water temperature is around 14°C, even on warm days

**Accommodation:** Plenty of options available, but book in advance during the summer months

**Additional Information:**

* Fistral Beach is a straight and wide, sandy beach backed by dunes, making it a popular spot for surfers and windsurfers
* The beach is home to a range of national and international surfing competitions throughout the year
* The beach is easily accessible by car, with a reasonably spacious car park available for a fee.
